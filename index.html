Wordle Rules and Guidelines

Objective

The objective of Wordle is to guess a secret five-letter word chosen by the game. This word is typically a common English word found in standard dictionaries.

Gameplay

  • You have six attempts to guess the secret word.
  • Each guess must be a valid five-letter English word.

Feedback Mechanism

After each guess, the game provides feedback in the form of colored squares:

  • A green square indicates that a letter in your guess is correct and in the correct position.
  • A yellow square indicates that a letter in your guess is correct but in the wrong position.
  • A gray square indicates that a letter in your guess is not present in the secret word.

Strategy

  • Use the feedback from previous guesses to eliminate possible words and make educated guesses.
  • Start with words that include common letters in English words.
  • Pay attention to the positions of correct letters to narrow down possibilities.
  • Avoid guessing proper nouns, hyphenated words, or abbreviations, as these are typically not allowed.

Winning

If you guess the secret word correctly within six attempts, you win the game.

Winning Wordle requires a combination of deduction, vocabulary, and strategy. Each successful guess brings you closer to identifying the secret word.

Fair Play

Wordle typically follows rules to ensure fair play and encourage players to guess legitimate English words.

  • Proper nouns, hyphenated words, and abbreviations are usually not allowed.
  • The chosen secret word is commonly a common English word found in standard dictionaries.
